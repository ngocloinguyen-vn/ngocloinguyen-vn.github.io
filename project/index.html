Ph.D. Research

NEEED - Nordic SEas sEDimentary anciEnt DNA: Sedimentary ancient DNA - a new proxy to investigate the impact of environmental change on past and present biodiversity in Nordic Seas. We will analyze sediment DNA in the six well-dated cores collected at Svalbard, Greenland and Jan Mayen shelves. By using multiple genetic markers, we follow changes in composition of different groups of organisms, from microalgae to protozoans and animals. We integrate historical biodiversity data with palaeoceanographic proxies to understand marine response to climate change.

INCREASE Project

Insight into the aNcient miCRobial divErsity from mArine SedimEnt: Marine sedimentary ancient DNA from Svalbard and Northern Europe. We use DNA preserved in sediments to reconstruct microbial communities in the Nordic Seas over the last few thousand years using metagenomics and genetic markers. This project integrates microbial diversity with environmental change from sediment records.

M.Sc. Research

My master thesis focused on methanotrophic bacteria, which utilize methane as their sole carbon and energy source. I applied cultivation-based and molecular approaches to isolate strains and determine phylogenetic positions, and used metagenomics to reconstruct genomes from environmental samples.
