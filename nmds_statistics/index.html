nMDS statistics

Date: July 23, 2025

1. ANOSIM (Analysis of Similarities)

What is the ANOSIM R statistic? R measures how well the groups are separated based on community composition.

It ranges from -1 to 1:

  • R ≈ 1: samples within groups are more similar to each other than to samples from other groups (strong separation).
  • R ≈ 0: no difference between groups (random grouping).
  • R < 0: more similarity between groups than within groups (rare in ecological data).

Significance values (p-values)

  • The p-value tests the null hypothesis that there is no difference between groups.
  • It is calculated by a permutation test (e.g., 999 permutations).
  • A low p-value (< 0.05) indicates that the observed R is unlikely due to chance, so groups are significantly different.

# Run ANOSIM for layer variable

anosim_layer <- anosim(m_com, grouping = metadata_id$layer, distance = "bray", permutations = 999)

2. PERMANOVA (Permutational Multivariate Analysis of Variance)

PERMANOVA is a non-parametric method used to test whether multivariate community composition differs among groups or along environmental gradients.

  • Tests whether the centroids (multivariate means) of groups differ in multivariate space.
  • Based on a distance matrix (e.g. Bray–Curtis).
  • Uses permutations instead of distributional assumptions.
  • PERMANOVA asks whether communities differ systematically among groups or predictors, not just by chance.
Interpretation of results
  • R²: fraction of community variation explained by the factor.
  • F value: strength of group separation relative to within-group variation.
  • p-value: statistical significance based on permutations.

# Run PERMANOVA for layer variable

adonis <- adonis2(m_com ~  layer, data = metadata_id, permutations = 999, method = "bray")

Interpretation of results
  • Degrees of freedom (Df)
    • Model Df = 3 → four layers compared (SUR, MID, BOT, SED).
    • Residual Df = 20 → remaining unexplained variation.
    • Total Df = 23 → total number of samples minus one.
  • Variation explained (R² = 0.4306)
    • 43.1% of total community variation is explained by sample layer. Indicates a strong structuring effect of layer on community composition.
  • Residual variation (R² = 0.5694)
    • 56.9% of variation is due to other unmeasured factors or stochasticity.
  • Pseudo-F statistic (F = 5.04)
    • High F value indicates that between-layer differences are much larger than within-layer differences.
  • Statistical significance (p = 0.001)
    • Differences in community composition among layers are highly significant.
    • The probability of observing this degree of separation by chance is < 0.1%.

Microbial community composition differs strongly and significantly across vertical layers, layer is a major driver of community structure in this dataset.

In general, NMDS was used to visualize differences in community composition based on dissimilarities matrix. Statistical significance of group differences was assessed using PERMANOVA (adonis2) and supported by ANOSIM. Homogeneity of multivariate dispersion was evaluated using PERMDISP to ensure that significant results reflected differences in community composition rather than within-group variability.
